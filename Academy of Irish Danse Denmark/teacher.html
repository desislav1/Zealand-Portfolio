Maria O'Flannery

Maria danced for the O'Neill School of Irish Dance from age 3 to 22. Maria's passions are Riverdance and children.She attended the Riverdance Masterclass in Dublin 2013, where she spent a week learning from and dancing with dancers from Riverdance. After school, Maria started to teach children. Maria has always enjoyed working with children and loves nothing more than to see kids grow and develop themselves through dance.

Alison O'Mahony

Alison danced for the O’Neill School of Irish Dance from age 4 to 24, enjoying success both nationally and overseas. While Alison’s main focus was competition, her passions are choreography and performance. Alison believes involvement in Irish dance is a fantastic way for everybody to create lifelong friendships and learn to work as a team while developing their coordination, athleticism, rhythm, and choreography abilities. Shannon’s vision is to create an inclusive environment where all students feel welcome and supported as they learn new skills and work to achieve their goals in dance. She is the teacher of the advanced class.

Aurora Hayes

Aurora danced for the O’Neill School of Irish Dance from the age of 6 to 22. Aurora’s passion for all things Irish is apparent, with her not only having been a competitive dancer, but also an accomplished Irish musician. Aurora has always enjoyed working with people. She believes Irish Dancing provides a fun and friendly opportunity for people of all ages to challenge themselves and master new things. She is the teacher for the professional dancers.
